📊 USD to PKR History (2000 – 2026)

The USD to PKR exchange rate is one of the most important financial indicators in Pakistan. It shows how strong or weak the Pakistani Rupee is compared to the US Dollar — directly affecting fuel prices, import costs, and daily expenses.

Below is a timeline of major milestones in USD to PKR history:

20011 USD ≈ 60 PKR — Pakistan had relatively stable inflation and balanced economy.
2008Dollar started rising due to political instability and global financial crisis.
2013Rate reached around 100 PKR — trade deficit worsened.
2018Dollar crossed 120 PKR — IMF bailout pressure increased.
2022Sharp depreciation — rate crossed 200 PKR due to inflation & global pressure.
2026Rate fluctuating at higher levels — ongoing economic challenges continue.

💡 Major Reasons Behind Rupee Devaluation

1. Trade Deficit

Pakistan imports more goods than it exports, increasing demand for USD.

2. Inflation

High inflation reduces the purchasing power of PKR, making it weaker.

3. Foreign Debt

Loan repayments require dollars, increasing constant pressure on PKR.

4. Political Instability

Uncertainty reduces investor confidence and weakens the economy.

5. Low Foreign Reserves

Low reserves make it difficult to stabilize currency rates effectively.

🏠 Impact on Daily Life

The dollar rate directly affects the life of every Pakistani citizen. When dollar rises:

  • ⛽ Fuel prices increase
  • ⚡ Electricity bills rise
  • 📱 Imported goods become expensive
  • 🍞 Food prices increase
  • 🛒 Overall mehngai barh jati hai

This reduces purchasing power and increases financial pressure on households across Pakistan.

🏦 Open Market vs Interbank Rate

There are two main exchange rates in Pakistan:

  • Interbank Rate: Used by banks for official transactions — controlled by State Bank of Pakistan
  • Open Market Rate: Used by exchange companies — reflects real public demand
💱 Open market rate is usually slightly higher than interbank rate due to profit margins and demand pressure.

🔮 USD to PKR Future Prediction (2026 & Beyond)

The future of USD to PKR depends on several economic factors:

  • Exports growth (IT, textiles)
  • Inflation control
  • Foreign investment
  • Political stability
  • IMF loan conditions

If Pakistan improves its economy, the rupee can stabilize. Otherwise, the dollar may continue to rise against PKR.
